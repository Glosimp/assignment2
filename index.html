⛽Energy in Australia🌏

The following visualisation will show how the energy inflow and outflow in Australia has changed over the course of 10 years. It also will showcase the change in population as the change in population also affect the energy consumption in the country.

Energy Consumption and Population Change in Australia📍

The following map shows how energy consumption changed in the span of 10 years. All figures are measured under the unit of PJ(petajoule) which is equivalent to 10^15 Joule [J]. The state with the high energy consumptions are namely, Western Australia, Queensland, New South Wales, and Victoria. Meanwhile, other states such as, Tasmania, Northern Terirtory, and South Australia, experienced barely any change in their energy consumption level.

Meanwhile, the following map shows how the population number in Australia has changed over the span of 10 years. The most populated state has always been New South Wales. New South Wales population increased by around one million in 10 years. Meanwhile other states like Western Australia, Tasmania, and Northern Terirtory experienced the least growth in population number.

💰 Energy Trade in Australia 💱

The following area graph shows the diffrent types of fuel traded by Australia. It consists of the likes of automotive gasoline, black coal, coke, crude oil, fuel oil, LPG, natural gas, and other petroelum products. Black coal is the biggest exported type of energy source (10323.501 PJ in 2020); meanwhile, crude oil is the biggest imported energy source (547.007 PJ in 2020)

Renewable and Non-Renewable Energy Change in Australia💡

The following line graphs show how the change of renewable and non-renewable energy usage change in Australia over the span of 10 years (from 2010 to 2020). The renewable energy consists of bagasse, biogas, wind, and hydro. Meanwhile, the non-renewable energy consists of aviation gasoline, LPG, natural gas, and electricity.

Non-Renewable and Renewable Energy Consumption Proportion

The following pie chart shows the proportion of renewable and non-renewale energy usage in Australia. Most states in Australia had a big proportion of the energy coming from a non-renewable energy soruce up until late 2010s. The state of Australia and Victoria had renewable energy above 25% of their total source in 2020.
